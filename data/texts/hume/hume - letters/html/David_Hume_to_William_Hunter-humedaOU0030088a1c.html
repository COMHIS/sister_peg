Title : David Hume to William Hunter1
Date : Wednesday, 24 October 1764
Written from:  Fontainebleau, Seine-et-Marne (department), Île-de-France, France
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0030088a1c

Dear Sir

Immediatly upon receiving yours2 of the 21st of August, I waited on Dr Astruc,3 and left with him your Letters, together with a Card for myself. I expected everyday, before I left Paris, that he woud have return'd my Visit, when I might have had some Conversation with him on the Subject of your Letter. I delay's acknowleging your Favour, till I coud be able to inform you of something more particular on that head. But as I never had an Opportunity of seeing the Doctor, I am not able to say any thing farther, than to assure you, that your Letter was deliver'd to him.

All this Family4 are well; but some of us in great Distress for the Departure5 of Lord Beauchamp, which indeed we have, all of us, great reason to regreat. I am

Dear Sir
Your most obedient and most humble Servant

David Hume

Fontainebleau 24 Octr 1764
