Title : David Hume to John Craufurd, 18th laird of Auchinames
Date : Monday, 29 August 1768
Written from:  Cirencester, Gloucestershire, England
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0030184a1c

Cirencester 129 of August 1768

Dear Crawfurd

The more I think, the more doubts I entertain concerning the Propriety of your writing at all to the Duke of Grafton,2 and the less reason I see of your throwing up your Office,3 in case the Answer be not favourable. His Grace passes for the most inflexible Man in the World; so that there are no hopes of his being influenc'd by any Letter[s.]a And as his late Coldness towards you proceeds [more]b from a Jealousy of Friendship than any thing else, it may rather be considerd as obliging, tho' unlucky. However, if you still persist in the Resolution, contrary to my Opinion, I beg to be informd before you put it [i]ntoc Execution: For I am fully perswaded my Friends will easily be able to procure it for me

I am here at a kind of classical Place, celebrated by Pope, Swift &c [a]ndd with a classical Man,4 who livd in Intimacy with these two wits and with Prior, Gay, Arbuthnot, Bolingbroke & every Person celebrated in his time. Both the Place and the Man are very agreeable, and the Weather is fine; my Appettite good; the Table plentiful, and the Wine excellent. Nothing is wanting to make the time pass as cheerfully as possible. I leave this towards the End of the Week, and go to Ragley:5 I shall be in Town about three weeks hence, where I hope to find you.

Yours sincerely

D. H.
