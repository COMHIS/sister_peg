Title : Adam Smith to David Hume
Date : Monday, 12 December 1763
Written from:  Glasgow, Glasgow (unitary authority), Scotland
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : Digital correspondence of Adam Smith
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/smitadOU0010098a1c

Glasgow, 12 Dec. 1763

My Dear Hume,

The day before I received your last letter1 I had the honour of a letter from Charles Townshend,2 renewing in the most obliging manner his former proposal that I should travel with the Duke of Buccleugh, and informing me that his Grace was to leave Eton at Christmas, and would go abroad very soon after that. I accepted the proposal, but at the same time expressed to Mr Townshend the difficulties I should have in leaving the University before the beginning of April, and begged to know if my attendance upon his Grace would be necessary before that time. I have yet received no answer to that letter,3 which, I suppose, is owing to this, that his Grace is not yet come from Eton, and that nothing is yet settled with regard to the time of his going abroad. I delayed answering your letter till I should be able to inform you at what time I should have the pleasure of seeing you . . . .4 I ever am, my dearest friend, most faithfully yours,

Adam Smith
