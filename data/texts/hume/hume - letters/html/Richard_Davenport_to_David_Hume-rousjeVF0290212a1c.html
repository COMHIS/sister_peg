Title : Richard Davenport to David Hume
Date : Wednesday, 14 May 1766
Written from:  Wootton Hall, Wootton, near Mayfield, Staffordshire, England
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0290212a1c

Sir

I came ona Friday1 & hadb the Satisfaction of Finding Mr Rouseau in perfect health, he seems to like the place, amuses himself with walking, when the weather is fair, if rainy he plays upon the harpsichord, or writes2 — isc very sociable & an excellent companion3; pray remember your promise of Calling, & of giving me as much notice as you Can, in about d10 daysd I shalle go to Davenport, Mr R— stays here, somehow be it at which house it chances, I beg leave to Assure you none will be more pleas'd to See Mr Hume than his Most Obedient

Hble Servt
Richd Davenport

Wootton May 14th 1766.
