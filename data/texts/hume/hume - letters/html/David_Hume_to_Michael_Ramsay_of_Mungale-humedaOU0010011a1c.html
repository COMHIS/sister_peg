Title : David Hume to Michael Ramsay of Mungale
Date : 17 / 24 / 31 March / 7 April 1732
Written from:  no location of writing
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0010011a1c

My Dear Mich.

I was very much surprizd & very sorry to hear from you, that we must not expect you soon. I wonder you make any stop about a horse, since we could send in for you, & as to your Letters from Jamey1 you would get them so much the sooner by Berwick. I hope there is no misfortune befallen him, that you need be so much upon the Guard. Our Folks goesa into Town agst the first of Aprile; I wish you could come out then, with a return of their horses, if your own be not right, & not wait for their coming out again, wch will be a fortnight after. I am in pretty good Health Just now & I think in better health these two months than for two Years before.2 I am afraid we shall never get a good long settled meeting together, till we must part, & then we must wait till Fortune join us. I thank you for your trouble about Baile.3 I hope it is a Book you will yourself find Diversion & Improvement in

March. Monday. 1732

Mr Mich. Ramsay. To the Care of Mrs Butler at her Lodgings in Hackerstons Wind Edinr
