Title : David Hume to Gilbert Stuart1
Date : Thursday, 23 December 1773
Written from:  Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0030202a1c

St Andrews Square 23 of December 1773.

Sir

I wish you woud check your Printer with some Severity for the Freedoms he uses; I suppose to divert himself. He has substituted2 the Name of Dr MacQueen,3 whom certainly I did not think of, instead of Dr Robertson, to whose Merit I meant to do some Justice. The last Paragraph which seems to be entirely his own, is also too high a Praise for a new Author like Dr Henry.4 But, if you want a few Sentences to fill up the Page, I have added them,5 and beg that you woud take care, that the Printer throw them off faithfully.6

I am Sir
Your most obedient Servant
David Hume
