Title : David Hume to Adam Smith
Date : Monday, 28 March 1763
Written from:  Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland
Written to : Glasgow, Glasgow (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : Digital correspondence of Adam Smith
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/smitadOU0010089b1c

Edinburgh, 28 Mar. 1763

Dear Smith

I was obligd to you both for your kind Letter and for the Opportunity which you afforded me of making Acquaintance with Mr Herbert, who appears to me a very promising young man.1 I set up a Chaise in May next, which will give me the Liberty of travelling about; and you may be sure a Journey to Glasgow will be one of the first I shall undertake. I intend to require with great Strictness an Account how you have been employing your Leizure; and I desire you to be ready for that purpose. Wo be to you, if the Ballance be against you. Your Friends here will also expect, that I should bring you with me. It seems to me very long since I saw you. I am Dear Smith

Yours most sincerely
David Hume
