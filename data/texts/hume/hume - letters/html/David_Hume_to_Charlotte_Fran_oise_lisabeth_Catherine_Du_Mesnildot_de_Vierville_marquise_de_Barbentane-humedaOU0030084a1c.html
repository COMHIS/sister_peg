Title : David Hume to Charlotte Françoise Élisabeth Catherine Du Mesnildot de Vierville, marquise de Barbentane1
Date : Friday, 8 June 1764
Written from:  Paris, Ville de Paris (department), Île-de-France, France
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0030084a1c

[Paris 8 June 1764]

I flatter myself, Madam, that I have found a sure and a safe Opportunity of commissioning from London the Books,2 which you did me the Honour to mention to me: But before I can do it with the requisite Exactness, I believe, that it will be necessary for me to see Monsr Du Pons.3 If you desire, therefore, that the Books shou'd be commissioned soon, you must be so good as to make that Gentleman allow me an Interview with him: If you be in no Hurry, we may delay it till I meet him at Compiegne.4 I have been this Evening to pay my Respects to you, and to wish you a good Journey.

And I have the Honour to be with the greatest Regard Madam
Your most obedient humble Servant

David Hume

Hotel de Brancas 58th of June 1764

Lord Hertford goes to Compiegne the 25th or 26th of the Month, and I accompany him thither.
