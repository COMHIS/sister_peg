Title : David Hume to James Edmonstoune of Newton
Date : between 1770 & 1774
Written from:  Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0030212a1c

[Edinburgh ]1

Mr Hume presents his Compliments to Signior Guiseppe. Hopes he is return'd home. Lord Kaims,2 Dr Blair,3 and Mr Hume have waited for him with great Impatience, and wish to pass to morrow in his Company, either at Newton or Blair-Drummond,4 whichever will be most convenient for him, viz., or videlicet, or to wit, at Newton, if he return to night or at Blair Drummond, if he return to morrow, so late as not to be able to give them timeous5 or rather timely Information of his Arrival.

Friday Afternoon
