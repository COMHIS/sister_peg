Title : Richard Davenport to David Hume
Date : Friday, 21 November 1766
Written from:  Davenport, near Congleton, Cheshire, England
Written to : Greater London, England, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0310190a1c

Dear Sir

I received the favour of yours1 & am very Sorry this ugly affair has given you so much trouble, I have not seen Rousseau of a long time & am ignorant whether he has seen Exposé Succinct &c. Will take care to communicate to him what you have desir'd; Surely he will be Surpriz'd to read Mr. D'Alembert's declaration2 As an unfortunate Stranger, I can't help pittying him, & heartily wish there had been no causea to have made the business publick. I have often said, that I never could find out any sort of reason, (at least none that had any weight with me) for his avoiding his Majesty's intended bounty to him — this is a kind of Philosophy which I have no comprehension of.

Sometime in January I purpose being in Town & hope to have the pleasure of meeting you there which will be extremely agreeable to

Dr Sir your Most oblidged Humble Servant

Richd Davenport

Davenport 21 Nr. 1766.

In my pasage to London I generally stay about ten days at Wootton, so expect to be there from the 15th of next, till near the end of the month . . . .
