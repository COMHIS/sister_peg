Title : Richard Davenport to David Hume
Date : Monday, 30 June 1766
Written from:  Davenport, near Congleton, Cheshire, England
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0020407a1c

[. . .] The receipt of Your two last gave me much uneasiness; which was augmented by some letters recd yesterday from Rousseau along with Yours directed to me at Wootton. Surely there must have been some excessive great mistakes, it appears to me an heap of confusion of which I can make neither head nor tail; his letter to you is perfectly astonishing, never any thing, was, so furious, so — — I protest I dont know what to call it [. . .] . Good God he must be most excessively out of the way, about this pension in short I have not patience to add one word more, till I hear what he can possibly have to say; & then Ill immediately acquaint you [. . .] .

Davenport Monday June 30, 1766.
