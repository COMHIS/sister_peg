Title : David Hume to Sir Robert Liston
Date : Tuesday, 5 July 1768
Written from:  Greater London, England, United Kingdom
Written to : Newliston, Kirkliston, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0030183a1c

Dear Sir1

I am sorry to tell you, that, by my last Accounts2 from Mr Turgot, he finds, that the Court of Parma are resolved to have a Catholic for a Professor, which is a Circumstance, as I have told him, very difficult to be obtain'd. I hope the Dissappointment gives you no Uneasyness. The Course of Studies, into which the Proposal has led you, will not be disagreeable, nor in any respects a Loss to you; and you have too much Prudence to set your Heart upon an Uncertainty. I did indeed think, that the Scheme woud have suited you very well, both in respect of Talents and Inclination.

I am with great Sincerity Dear Sir
Your most obedient humble Servant

David Hume

London 5 July 1768
