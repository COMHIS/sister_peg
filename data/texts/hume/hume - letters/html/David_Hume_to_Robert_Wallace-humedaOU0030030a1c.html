Title : David Hume to Robert Wallace
Date : Sunday, 10 October 1751
Written from:  Glanderston, near Glasgow, Glasgow (unitary authority), Scotland
Written to : Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0030030a1c

Sir

I receiv'd your very obliging Letter.a The Discourses are at present in the Press, & will be publish'd as soon as they are printed off & sufficiently dry; which I fancy will require two or three Months. Mr Kincaid,1 who is printing them, will best be able to inform you. Why cannot all the World entertain different Opinions about any Subject, as amicably as we do?

I am Sir
Your most obedient humble Servant

David Hume

Glanderston 29 Septr. 1751
