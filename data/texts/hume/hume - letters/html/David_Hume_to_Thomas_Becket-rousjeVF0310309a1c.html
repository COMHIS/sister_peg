Title : David Hume to Thomas Becketa
Date : Saturday, 27 December 1766
Written from:  no location of writing
Written to : Greater London, England, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0310309a1c

Sir

I have no Objection to your joining M. de Voltaire's Letterb 1 to mine: You havec certainly a Right to dispose of them as you think Proper.

I cannot imagine that a Pieced wrote on so silly a Subject as mine will ever come to a second Edition; but if it shoud, please order the following Corrections to be made

Page viii of the Advertisement, in the Note say The original Letters of M. Rousseau will be lodged &c2

Page 4. Read Hic domus hac patria est3

Page 6. The Passage of the Notes which names Mde de Verdelin must be suppress'd4.

Page 18. Read eon condition onlye that the Affair shoud remain a kind of Secret5.

Page 21. instead of out of regard to me, read, agreeably to the usual Politeness & Humanity of his Character6.

Page 34. There is a Note omitted here, which shoud be restord from the French Edition7.

Page 38. In the Note instead of is equally contemptible read, is equally mistaken8.

Page 70. Add to my Note, these Words: Since the publication of the first Edition, I receivd a Letter from a Foreigner, residing in London9, who expresses his extreme Surprize at Mr Rousseau's ascribing this Piece to me, together with that mentiond in page 65. For this Gentleman, whom I never saw, confesses that he wrote both for his Amusement: He thene conceal'd his Name, because he did not care to appear as the Author of such trifles: But he very genteely offersf to allow me to publish his Letter, if I think it necessary for the Vindication of my Character: But really I do not think it necessary, and I do not judgeg it proper to take the Gentleman from his Retreat by giving his Name to the Public. Nothing but new Defiances on the part of M. Rousseau shall oblige me to make use of the Freedom, which the Gentleman allows me.

Page 71 Omitt the Translator's Note10.

Page 79 Add to my Note11, these Words. If M. Rousseauh consult his Plutarch, he will find, that when Themistocles fled into Persia, Xerxes was so pleas'd with this Event, that he was heard to exclaim several times in his sleep, I have Themistocles, I have Themistocles12. Why will not M. Rousseau understand my Exclamation in the same Sense?

Page 86 Omitt Mde de Boufflers Name13.

Page 94 read on whom the Public Suspicions have never fallen14.

I am Sir Your most humble Servant

David Hume
