Title : Richard Davenport to David Hume
Date : Monday, 30 June 1766
Written from:  Davenport, near Congleton, Cheshire, England
Written to : Greater London, England, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0290291a1c

Dear Sir,

[para. 1]

I have [not]b yet been at Wootton, being prevented by my dear Grandson's1 having a worm fever; he is now a good deal better, so if c the weather will permitc, I intend d going tomorrowd.

[para. 2]

The receipt of Your two2 last gavee me f much uneasinessf; which was augmentedc by some letters g recd yesterdayg from Rousseauh 3 alongi with Yours directed for me at Wootton4. Surely there must j have been some excessive great mistakesj, itk appears to me an heap of confusion of which I can make neither head nor tail; l his letter to you is perfectly astonishing, never any thing, was, so furious, so — I protest I dont know what to call it, I long to see him, he certainly will tell some reason or other that could induce him to write in that manner. Till I have seen himm I can give no sort of answer to Your Queries, as he never spoke one syllable to me about any difference at alll. n I can't possibly before Saturdays5 post, as in this part of the country, we have only 3 days in a week to send letters to Town.n

[para. 3]

o You desird me to burn the duplicate6 after reading, that signifies nothing, for I can send you the other which I recd yesterday from Wootton.o

[para. 4]

Good God he must be mostp excessively out of the way, about this pension, in shortq I have not patience to add one word more, till I r hear what he can possibly have to say; & then I'll immediately acquaint your

[para. 5]

I directlys gave orders for the plough, t which will very soon be made, as tis quite simple, & not more work in it than a comon onet.

[para. 16]

u I can't help being troubled at seeing your Uneasiness, & will with great pleasure do all in my power to assist in freeing you from it, at least I'll do my best endeavours.u

u I am Your Most Obedient

Hble Sert
Richd Davenport

Davenport Munday
June 30. 1766.u
