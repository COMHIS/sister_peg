Title : David Hume to Richard Davenport
Date : Sunday, 23 November 1766
Written from:  Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland
Written to : Davenport, Congleton, Cheshire, England, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0310204a1c

[le 23 novembre 1766] a

Dear Sir

I doubt not but you have already got a Copy of that Pamphlet, which with great Reluctance I was obliged to allow my Friends at Paris to publish with regard to this miserable Affair between Rousseau and me; and I cannot doubt of the Judgement you wou'd form of it. Two days ago, I received a Letter of which I send you a Copy enclosed1. The References are to the Paris Edition; but the English Translation has the same Passages. The pages 85, 86 of the Paris Edition correspond to 65, 66 of the Translation; and the pages 9(d)–(d)6 to 7(d)–(d)4 of the Translation; I beg of you to transmit the enclos'd to Rousseau2. The Reason is, that my Friends imagineb, he is madc enough to think of replying and continuing the Controversy: He will see, from this Instance, what weapons I have still in my hand to confound him, if he shoud make such an Attempt, and if I shoud think it worth while to take any farther Notice of itd.

I am Dear Sir

Your most obedient humble Servant David Hume

Edinburgh 27 of Novr 1766a
