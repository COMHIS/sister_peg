Title : David Hume to père Jean Bernard Le Blanc, abbé Le Blanc
Date : Friday, 15 June 1764
Written from:  Paris, Ville de Paris (department), Île-de-France, France
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0010445a1c

Sir

I delayd sending you the Books which I receivd a few days ago, intending every day to call upon you and to endeavour making myself Amends for my Misfortune in meeting with you so seldom. I shall lay hold of every Opportunity with Pleasure that may bring us together, and particularly shall give you warning the first day I go to Chatillon,1 that I may have the Honour of conveying you thither. The Price of Edwards2 is stated to me at six Guineas with seven Shillings for Box, Package, Freight & Carriage from London. I think it dear, but it seems to be well ornamented. I am

Sir
Your most obedient humble Servant

David Hume

Hotel de Brancas rue de l'Université. 15 of June 1764

A Monsieur Monsieur L'Abbé le Blanc Historiographe des batimens du Roi rue neuve des bons enfans.
