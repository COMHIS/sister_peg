Title : David Hume to John Home of Ninewells1
Date : Sunday, 2 February 1766
Written from:  Greater London, England, United Kingdom
Written to : Ninewells, Chirnside, Scottish Borders (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0280266a1c

Dear Brother

[. . .]a

You will have heard by this time, that I have brought over with me the famous Rousseau, the most singular Man surely in the World. He applyd to me last Summer to take him under my Protection in England2, as he calld it; but in the mean while, he was chac'd out of Switzerland, and came to Strasbourg, with an Intention of going to the King of Prussia, who pressd him earnestly to live with him. At Strasbourg my Letter reachd him3, making him an Offer of all my Services, upon which he turn'd short, and having obtained the King b of France'sb Passport came and joind me at Paris. I have liv'd with him ever since: He is very modest, mild, well-bred, gentle-spirited and warm-hearted Man, as ever I knew in my Life: He is also in appearance very sociable: I never saw a man who seems better calculated for good Company nor who seems to take more Pleasure in it; c yet is hec absolutely determind to retire and board himself in a Farmer's House among the Mountains of Wales b for the sake of Solitudeb. He has refus'd a Pension from the King of Prussia and Presents from hundreds: I have been offerd great Sums for him, if I cou'd have prevaild on him to accept of them: Yet till within these three Months he was in absolute Beggary: He has now about 70 pounds a [year]d which he has acquird by a Bargain, for his Works. It is incredible the Enthusiasm for him in Paris and the Curiosity in London. I prevaild on him to go to the Play-house, in order to see Garrick, who placd him in a Box opposite the King and Queen4. I observd their Majestys to look at him, more than at the Players. I shoud desire no better Fortune than to have the Privilege of showing him to alle I please. The hereditary Prince5 payd him a Visit a few days ago; and I imagine the Duke of York calld on him one Evening when he was abroad. I love him much and shall separate from him with muchf Regreat.

I am Dear Brother

Yours sincerely
David Hume

London 2d Feby 1766
