Title : David Hume to Richard Davenporta
Date : Saturday, 17 October 1767
Written from:  Greater London, England, United Kingdom
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0340148a1c

Dear Sir

I doubt not but you are desirous of having Accounts of your wild Philosopher as you us'd to call him; Mr Walpole has return'd from Franceb a few days ago, who tells me, that he left him at Cliché, a Place within a League of Paris1, where he lives in a very retir'd and obscure manner. I fancy he may pay you another Visit; For this Tranquillity is not what he desires, and if he cannot get Persecution, he will at least seek after Variety. May I use the Freedom to put you in mind of bringing up with you all the Letters he wrote on his Departure from you and afterwards? there are some who are of Opinion, that the letter lately publishd2 is not genuine, but is rather contriv'dc as a Banter upon him. I am Dear Sir

Your most obedient and most humble

Servant
David Hume.

London 17 of Oct 1767
