Title : David Hume to Henry Home, Lord Kames
Date : Sunday, 12 July 1739
Written from:  Ninewells, Chirnside, Scottish Borders, Scotland
Written to : Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/humedaOU0030006a1c

My Dear Sir

I hope you always esteem yourself more oblig'd to me, when I send you Papers I do not approve of, than where I send you those I think more tolerable; since there may be a Share of Vanity in the latter Case, which can have no Part in the former. I have a strong Suspicion against the present Packet. One of the Papers will be found very cold; & the other be esteem'd somewhat sophystical.1 However I communicate them to you; because I may possibly be mistaken. I remember Boileau2 enumerating the Advantages which may be met with in consulting a judicious Friend concerning aa man's Performances, says among other things

Lui seul eclairçira vos doutes ridicules
De votre esprit tremblant levera les scrupules.

I thank your for your Manuscript,3 which I have scarce had Time to look into, being very late at Redbraes-Castle4 last Night, & being oblig'd to dispatch away this in the Morning.b I am more & more convinc'd that Lady Jane's5 Advice will be serviceable to us; tho' I am afraid, that my Lord Marchmont's6 Arrival will deprive us of such easy Opportunitys of speaking to her as cou'd be wisht. He is expected in a few Days.

I have committed some Mistakes in former Papers by trusting to my Memory. I have met with the Story of Diogenes in Cicero told in a better way. I suppose that instead of the Hydrogynes, I shou'd have said the Androgynes of Plato.7

I shall be in Town about the End of next Week.c I suppose your Friends have been lazy the last Vacation, that I have not heard of them.8 I am
Yours sincerely

David Hume

Ninewells. Sunday July. 1. 1739
