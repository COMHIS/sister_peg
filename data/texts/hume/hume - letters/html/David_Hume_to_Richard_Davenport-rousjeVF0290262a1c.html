Title : David Hume to Richard Davenporta
Date : Thursday, 19 June 1766
Written from:  Lisle Street, Leicester Fields, City of Westminster, Greater London, England
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0290262a1c

Dear Sir

Your guest is not ab little whimsical. He made me no Reply, when I endeavour'd to perswade him to accept of His Majesty's Bounty, even thoc a Secret: Upon which I made a new Application to General Conway, that he would prevail on his Majesty to depart from thisd Condition. The General only requires, as is reasonable, that Mr Rousseau shou'd promise to accept, in case the King be pleas'd to bestow on him a Pension publickly. I have wrote him ethe enclosde 1 for thatf Purpose, in case he be with you at Davenport. I have also wrote to Wooton under Direction to your Steward, in case he be at that Place. Ifg he be at Wooton, you may open & read & burn the enclosd

I am Dear Sir

Your most obedient & most humble Servant
David Hume

Lisle Street
Leicester Fields
19 June 1766
