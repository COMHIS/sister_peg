Title : David Hume to Sir John Pringle
Date : Tuesday, 13 August 1776
Written from:  Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus

Edinburgh 13 Aug. 1776

Dear Sir

You saw me to great Advantage when I was at London, the Journey had a little revived me; which with some remains of Health and Strength, made you hope that a Cure was still practicable; But Affairs change, every day and rapidly, to the worse: My Appetite is totally gone, my Strength decays, and every bad Symptom visibly augments; so that I can now look only for a near approaching Dissolution. One would little regret Life, were it not the Experience of such good Friends, as you, whom one must leave behind them. Mais, helas! on ne laisse que des mourans: as Ninon l'Enclos1 said on her Death-bed. Death appears to me so little terrible on his Approaches, that I scorn to quote Heroes and Philosophers as Examples of Fortitude: a Woman of Pleasure, who, however, was also a Philosopher, is sufficient. I embrace you, Dear Sir, and probably for the last time.

David Hume.
