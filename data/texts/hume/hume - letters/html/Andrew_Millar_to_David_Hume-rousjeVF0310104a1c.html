Title : Andrew Millar to David Hume1
Date : Sunday, 2 November 1766
Written from:  Kew Green, Kew, Richmond upon Thames (borough), Greater London, England
Written to : Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0310104a1c

[. . .] I will tell yo honestly that I was much hurt yesterday wt yours to Mr Strahan2, w₶ he showed me when in Town, about his, Messrs Beckett or Cadell's3 being imployed by you in Publishing this absurd Dispute of Rousseau wt yo, as yo imagined it would not be worth my while. Can you imagine any thing however so trifling in w₶ yr name is concerned, not worth my while? surely I never did. a Dr. Lowth4 thought differentlya in a more delicate affair and even b or lessb in point of value. In truth the money w₶ will be got I do not value, but in the eye of the world where I have so cordial a friendship, to see other names and not mine looks as though yo were offended. [. . .] Strahan and Beckett divide the Spoil unless yo interpose. It is a painfull thing to loose a favourite. I leave it to yr own Judgement to act as yo see fit. I think it wd be no disadvantage to b you tob have my name and Mr Cadells.

If I had ever dreamed of this Publication, I shd have wrote to yo but indeed I did not. [. . .]

I am Dr Sr

yr Most obedt hum sert
And: Millar

Kew Green 2d Novr 1766
