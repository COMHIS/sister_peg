Title : Adam Smith to David Hume
Date : Sunday, 4 November 1764
Written from:  Toulouse, Haute-Garonne (department), Midi-Pyrénées, France
Written to : Paris, Ville de Paris (department), Île-de-France, France, Europe
Editorial project : Digital correspondence of Adam Smith
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/smitadOU0010103a1c

Toulouse, 4 Nov. 1764

My dear friend,

This letter will be delivered to you by Mr Urquhart,1 the only man I ever knew that had a better temper than yourself. You will find him most perfectly amiable. I recommend him in the most earnest manner to your advice and protection. He is not a man of letters and is just a plain, sensible, agreeable man of no pretentions of any kind, but whom you will love everyday better and better. I ever am

My Dear Friend, Most faithfully yours
Adam Smith
