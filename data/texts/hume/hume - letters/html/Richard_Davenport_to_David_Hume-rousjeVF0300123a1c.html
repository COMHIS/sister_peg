Title : Richard Davenport to David Hume
Date : Saturday, 19 July 1766
Written from:  Davenport, near Congleton, Cheshire, England
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0300123a1c

Dear Sir

I have not Since I wrote1, seen Rousseau, I fear you form a true Judgment of him, to mention Dreams, Suspicions &c. Good God, in one half quarter of a small sheet I could have easily Summ'd up all, but fairly own I was asham'd to put em upon paper, if I have the pleasure of Seeing you perhaps you'll hear Some Anecdotes that will (I cant say after what he has wrote) Surprize you, for in troth they are quite of the Same Stamp. They are some small Altercations which happened between him Madslle & our Women.

I am Dear Sir

Your Most Obedient
Hble Sert
Richd Davenport

Davenport July
19th 1766.

a This place is not a bit out of your waya
