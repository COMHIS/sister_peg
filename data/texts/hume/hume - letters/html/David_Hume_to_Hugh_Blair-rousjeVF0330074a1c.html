Title : David Hume to Hugh Blair
Date : Wednesday, 20 May 1767
Written from:  Greater London, England, United Kingdom
Written to : Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0330074a1c

[. . .] You may perhaps have heard, that Rousseau has elop'd from Mr Davenport without giving any Warning; leaving all his Baggage except Mdlle, about thirty pounds in Davenport's hands, and a Letter on the Table abusing him in the most violent Terms, insinuating that he was in a Conspiracy with me to ruin him1. He took the Road to London; but was missing for about a Fortnight: At last he emerges ata Spalding in Lincolnshireb; whence he writes a Letter to the Chancellor2, informing him that the bad Usage he had met with in England made it absolutely necessary for him to evacuate the Kingdom, and desiring his Lordship to send him a Guard to escort him in Safety to Dover; this being the last Act of Hospitality, he will desire of the Nation. He is plainly mad; tho I believe not more so, than he has been all his Life. The Pamphlet you mention3 was wrote by one as mad as himself, and it was believd at first to be Tristram Shandy, but proves to be one Fuseli, an Engraver. He is a fanatical Admirer of Rousseau, but owns he was in the wrong to me. The Pamphlet I sent you was wrote by an English Clergyman whom I never saw4; a man of Character, and rising in the Church: For which Reason, it is more prudent in me to conceal his Name. When woud you have done so much for me? [. . .]

I am Dear Sir

Yours sincerely
David Hume

London. 20 of May 1767
