Title : David Hume to Jean Jacques Rousseaua
Date : Friday, 28 February 1766
Written from:  no location of writing
Written to : Chiswick, Greater London, England, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2008
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/rousjeVF0280341a1c

[le 28 février? 1766] b

Dear Sir

I shall call on you to morrow half an hour past twoc in the Afternoon, in order to attend you to Town.

I have made Enquiries concerning the Viscount d'Ars1. I find, he is alive and in health. He is a Widower; and engaged in a Law-suit, which seems of a very delicate Nature, and of an uncertain Issue: It is of Importance; but a full Explication of it, as it wou'd be difficult for me, is not material to Mde de Verdelin.

I hope you remember your Promise, that you are to give me the Receit for playing the Trick of Hocus Pocus, which you mention in your Lettres de la Montagne and which you play'd when at Venice2. I have already promised it, on your Faith, to the Countessd of Ailesbury3, who is impatient to know it. I am, with the greatest Sincerity Dear Sir Your most obedient Servant

David Hume

Friday Morningb
