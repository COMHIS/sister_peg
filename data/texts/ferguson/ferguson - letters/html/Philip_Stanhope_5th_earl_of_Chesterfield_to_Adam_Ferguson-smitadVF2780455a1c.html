Title : Philip Stanhope, 5th earl of Chesterfield to Adam Ferguson1
Date : Wednesday, 22 October 1777
Written from:  Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland
Written to : Edinburgh, Edinburgh (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : SVEC Compendium
Published online : 2016
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/smitadVF2780455a1c

October 1777

Dear Sir I am this Moment arrived with Lady Chesterfield and three of my Friends at this place and take the very first opportunity of Informing you of my Arrival and at the same time to say that I shall be Glad to settle the Affair about the pension a-(meaning the said Annuity of £200 a Year Agreed to be paid to pet[itioner] during his natural Life as hereinbefore ment[ione]d)-a with you in the which hitherto I had Acted Improperly thro' bad Council and make no doubt but that I shall settle it entirely to your Satisfact. in five Minutes Conversation — We are at Paxtons in the Grass Market I am Dr. Sir Yours Sincerely

Chesterfield
