London, 24 February 1767

Dear Ferguson

I happen'd yesterdayb to visit a person three hours after [xxx]c a [xxx]d Copy of your Performance1 was open'd for the first timee in London. It was by Lord Mansfield2. I accept the Omen of its future Success. He was extremely pleas'd with it; said it was perfectly well wrote; assured me, that he woud not stop a moment till he had finish'd it, and recommended it Strongly to the Perusal of the Archbishop of Yorke3, who was present.

Tho' I set out with Reluctance I do not repent my Journey4. Direct me at Miss Elliotts5 in Brewer's Street. I have not seen Smith6: Judge of my hurry.

Yours Sincerely

David Hume
