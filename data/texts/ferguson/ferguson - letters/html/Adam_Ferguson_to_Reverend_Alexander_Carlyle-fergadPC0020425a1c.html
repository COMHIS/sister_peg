Title : Adam Ferguson to Reverend Alexander Carlyle
Date : between October 1797 & January 1798
Written from:  no location of writing
Written to : Musselburgh, East Lothian (unitary authority), Scotland, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2013
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/fergadPC0020425a1c

[folio 1 recto]

‘your Friend Collingwood1 is Certainly a man of excellent sense, I shall say nothing of Teneriffe false intelligence2 might have misled any body: but the Bombardment of Cadiz could serve no purpose but to disstress & to Alienate a Prople who I believe in their hearts are disposed to be our friends. I respect Mr Blacket3 greatly & am sorry not to have seen him. You do not mention J. Homes health & I am in hopes that besides trouts he may get Some Vigour at Longfarmacks4 as I yet here at Hallyards. The account of your Ducal Neighbours5 is very agreeable all to the Dutchess' complaints for which I grieve very Sincerely but hope they are not very great. Mr Pitts Supposed financial Sch[eme]a 6 sounds well. Every one I think should be willingb to buy up his own Land Tax at 20 yrsc [pur]chase &c &c. But let him not undervalue his Enn[emy]d for want of Finance, there is nobody can tell how much they may still do without it. And let him not Scold the French Directory for refusing Peace7 they cannot make peace & they dare not attempt it. The Seeds in which I have been dissappointed this year & for some years past are not better than Carrots & Turnips8: Robt Douglass at Galashiels9 has under takene for my field Turnips. And I shall move heaven & Earth rather than miss carrots another year. The kind I want is the Large Red Flander Carrotf if you know any seeds man to be trusted with such a Commission let me know. They talk to me of horn Carrots orange Carrots &c &c but I like hemlocks as well . . . .

g-I have read more of theh Robisons book & think he has disclosed a scene of Villany to much good purpose. As to free Masonry I have been long apprised of the power of Arcana10 in gulling mankind, & what a shallow fellow he is who has no Secrets-g
