Title : Adam Ferguson to James Chalmer
Date : Friday, 6 April 1792
Written from:  no location of writing
Written to : Abingdon Street, City of Westminster (borough), Greater London, England, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2013
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/fergadPC0020349a1c

[folio 1 recto]

Dear Sir

Above you have a Certificate which I hope will entitle you to receive my half years appointment from Lord Chesterfields Banker. You will recollect the Bussiness on which I have lately been at London1. It was so far advanced at my Departure that I left my Son Joseph Ferguson possessed of an Appointment of Cadet in the Corps of Artillery on the Bengale Establishment and [folio 1 verso] of an Order to Captain Dempster of the [xxx]a to give him his Passage to India. I left him in the hands of Captain Dempster & of some Friends in the City for this purpose paying all Costs for Passage & Necessarys on the Voyage or on his arrival. But in case any trifle should remain from oversights, delay or Other Accident I left with him a letter to you requesting that you would be so good as Supply him to [folio 2 recto] my Account, & I now renew the same request. I am Dear Sir

your most Obedient & most humble Servant

Adam Ferguson

Edinburgh April 6th 1792
