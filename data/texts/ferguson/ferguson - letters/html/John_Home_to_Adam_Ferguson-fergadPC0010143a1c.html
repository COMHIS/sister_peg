London 2 May 1776

My Dear Adam

Smith would inform you [xxx]a of our condition & Ninewells1 a second time to whom David wrote2 upon the road. I deferred my despatch till we should reach the place which we did last night in much better plight than any body expected. Sir John Pringle is of opinion that David will move but he has taken till to morrow to deliver his Decisions & Observations for the proceedings of the Patient. David in reality mended every day [folio 1 verso] not that he had not some changes for the first two hundred miles, & a [xxx] betterb day & a [xxx]c but for the last six days. He went on without any back going. About three days before we gott to London Colen3 came from Pulteny [xxx]d to [xxx]e and told me with great joy that Mr. Hume was certainly going to be well for that he complained of cold & was to sleep that night with an additional covering coveringf which he did & continued to do. This Mr Colen Mr David and I agree to be a remarkable good sign. [folio 2 recto] David observed that he was beginning to be like other people. If I can guess, Pringle will send him to Bath & Bristol for he seems determined not to leave Greatg Britain. I shall write you again to morrow. I [xxx]h pass my time more agreeably than with David uponi the road, nor did I ever see himj better company. Mr Colen is positive that if I had not joined him he would have turned back. As to that I cannot say, but as Fleutcher4 says of the Lord that I did him no evil.

I am with my compliments to Mrs Ferguson Dear Adam ever yours

J. Home

turn over
a Vessel is arrived with an account of the troops having quelled Boston. They [xxx xxx]k without loss of one man & left only two mortars one of which they rended useless & they threw the other into a deep well.

[It is]l thought they are gone to Rhode Island
