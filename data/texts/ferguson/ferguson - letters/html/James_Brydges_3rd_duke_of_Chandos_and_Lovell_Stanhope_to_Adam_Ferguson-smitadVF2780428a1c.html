Title : James Brydges, 3rd duke of Chandos and Lovell Stanhope to Adam Ferguson1
Date : Saturday, 17 June 1775
Written from:  Minchenden House, Enfield (borough), Greater London, England
Written to : no location of receipt
Editorial project : SVEC Compendium
Published online : 2016
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/smitadVF2780428a1c

Minchenden House, 17 June 1775

Sr. Lord Chesterfield hav[in]g Com[m]unicated to us a Joint Letter from Lord Stanhope Sr. George Saville and Mr. Hewitt2 dated the 3d. Inst acquainting his Lordship that they found themselves under such difficulties and Embarrassmts. in the Execution of the Trust in which they were Engaged as reduced them to the necessity of resigning the office of his Guardns. and desiring his Lordship to make choice in their stead of such other p[er]son or p[er]sons as might appear to him more useful than was in their power[,] his Lordship applied to us to accept the care of his person during the Short time of his Minority[,] w[hi]ch we have accepted and finding Mr. De Saint Germain (a Gent[leman] who Travelled with Lord George Cavendish3) had been thot. of by St. George to accompany Lord Chesterfield in his future Tour Abroad and finding that Mr. De Saint Germain is a proper p[er]son for that Employmt. we have Engaged him from the 24th Instant from w[hi]ch time your attendance on his Lordship seems to us unnecessary[.] Mr. Stanhope acquainted you of this on Tuesday last[,] the day after Lord Chesterfield applied to us but as you have expressed a desire that we shod. ratifye it in wiritng we now do it and are Sr. Your Obedt. Servts.

Chandos
L: Stanhope.
