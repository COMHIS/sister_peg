[folio 1 recto]

London August 9th 1780

My dear Sir

I received both your letters; and immediately applied. Lord Sandwich1 is out of town; as soon as he returns, which will be in a day or two, this letter to Pureia 2 will be sent. Mr Drummond3 will, at the same time, write to Provost Nairne4. Assurances are given to me that every place vacant, or to beb vacant, in any or all the Citiesc will be given to C.C.'s5 recommendation, and to his onlyd.

This matter is fixed; and assurances may be accordingly given. Blank commissions, or warrants, are out of rule and are, therefore, out of the Question. We knew, before hand, of the Lord Advocate's6 predilections; but as he cannot be a friend, we have reason to think he will not be an enemy. An Express, as you mention, will, very probably, be sent with the letters. Mr Harley's7 letter, or answer, is not yet arrived; as he is in Herefordshire ———

yours &c &c &c
