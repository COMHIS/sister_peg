Date : Friday, 16 September 1808
Written from:  Hallyards on Manor Water, near Peebles, Scottish Borders, Scotland
Written to : Brompton, near Chatham, Medway (unitary authority), Kent, England, Europe
Editorial project : EE founding corpus
Published online : 2013
Crossref DOI : http://dx.doi.org/10.13051/ee:doc/fergadPC0020509a1c

[folio 1 recto]

Hallyards 16 Septr
1808

My Dear Friend

I went into the Casea 2 drawn up by your Friends at London, as soon as I received it, And very highly esteem their b-[xxx]-b part only in it, as well as the Ground That was laid for them, And attempted to write to you immediately; sed ter cecidere manusc 3. I should be sorry to break upon that confidence in Mankind & in Justice which has been a Characteristic of you[r] life. At the same time I wished to guard you against the wounds of Dissappointment in this Appeal to Justice, and Candour. What we have to dread is no[t] a direct negative to our Claims, but the delays which Insidious malice may [folio 1 verso] still procure & with a more cruel Effect than any immediate decision could have. These were topics on which I could easily say too much and on which after I had written I wished to retract. In short, I applaud your confidence in all worthy men: but would join with al[l] indifference to those who are Otherwise. When Alexander saw Diogenes4 he felt immediately that next to Conquering the World thatd was the very part he should Act. Here is a Tub more roomy than that of Diogenes and it would be joyous for me to see you every moment of My Life.

You mention a letter of Sir John Dalling* but did not inclose it. If it be material that Fordyce should see it: please send it to him.

I am My Dear Friend
your most affectionately
Adam Ferguson
